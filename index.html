Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about how our QR codes work or what styles you should choose? We have answers.

When you build a Resume or Menu Card, QRStudio encodes your form fields into a compressed format and passes it as a link inside the QR code. When scanned by a phone, the QR code redirects the scanner to our interactive preview page which unpacks the data and presents a gorgeous, responsive, digital page. Best of all, it is serverless and works anywhere!

No, the QR codes generated with QRStudio are static. They contain the embedded details directly (like WiFi data, URL links, or email addresses). Because the data is stored directly inside the code, they will never expire and will work forever.

If you're using the QR code on websites, digital screens, or emails, PNG is perfect. If you're printing the QR code on banners, posters, mugs, or business cards, we recommend downloading SVG because it's a vector format and can scale to any size without losing crispness.

Our uploader allows you to upload a file (up to 1MB) which we convert into local data for immediate device testing. However, for a PDF QR code to work for external users scanning your physical QR code, we highly recommend inputting a public cloud URL (like Google Drive, Dropbox, or OneDrive share link) so scanners can download the PDF over the internet.

To ensure high scan reliability, always maintain high contrast between the foreground dots and the background. Avoid picking a background color that is too similar to the dots. A darker foreground on a lighter background scans the most consistently across all smartphones.
